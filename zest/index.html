2. PROBLEM

An Additional Shipping Method

Add an additional shipping method to the corporate gift shop. Currently, the corporate gift shop intakes multiple addresses from gift purchaser and ships gifts directly to gift recipients. Gift purchaser wants the option to ship their entire order to one address.

3. PROCESS

Sketches

Each team member generated two end-to-end flow sketches exploring various implementations of bulk shipping. Consistent elements emerged, such as placing the new shipping method under the delivery section. However, the method of selecting bulk shipping varied, prompting further discussion.

Initial sketches outlined various design concepts. We emphasized displaying shipping costs prominently and opted for a dropdown menu to distinguish between physical and electronic gifts, aligning with existing options. Iterations focused on minimizing clicks and ensuring clarity in the order process.

Lo-fi

We finalized design decisions based on wireframes, including:

Client Feedback

Feedback from Zest's founding designer Christine Pun prompted reconsideration of our design choices. We reverted to a single shipping method for individual addresses for clarity. Additional feedback emphasized the importance of mobile optimization and the potential for user testing to validate design decisions.

Iteration 1

We initially put the bulk shipping option under the existing "ship to recipients' addresses" as a way of grouping both physical delivery methods. However, Christine's feedback informed us that shipping to one address is completely different the current shipping to recipients method.
